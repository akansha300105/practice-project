Northeast India: A Tapestry of Culture and Cuisine

The Northeast is often called the "Seven Sisters and a Brother", referring to the seven states (Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura) and the newer addition, Sikkim. This region is geographically isolated, connected to the rest of India by a narrow strip of land (the Siliguri Corridor), which helped preserve its distinct identity and traditions.

Assam

Assam's culture is a blend of indigenous tribes and rich traditions like Bihu dance and music, while its food is flavorful yet simple, featuring dishes like khar, masor tenga, and rice with mustard oil.

Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is known for its vibrant tribal culture, scenic mountains, and unique cuisines like bamboo shoot dishes and apong (rice beer), reflecting its deep-rooted indigenous traditions.

Nagaland

Nagaland is renowned for its diverse Naga tribes, vibrant festivals like Hornbill, and bold, spicy cuisine featuring smoked meats, bamboo shoots, and fermented delicacies like axone.

Manipur

Manipur is rich in classical dance, martial arts, and scenic beauty, with a distinct cuisine that features fermented fish (ngari), spicy stews like eromba, and fresh herbs.

Mizoram

Mizoram is a peaceful hill state known for its harmonious tribal lifestyle, vibrant festivals, and light, healthy cuisine with dishes like bai and sawhchiar made from local herbs and meat.

Tripura

Tripura blends tribal and Bengali cultures, celebrated through its traditional music, temples, and a unique cuisine featuring fermented fish (berma), bamboo shoots, and rice-based dishes.

Meghalaya

Meghalaya—the "Abode of Clouds"—is known for its lush landscapes, tribal heritage, and hearty cuisine like jadoh and dohneiiong, rooted in Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo traditions.

Sikkim

Sikkim is a serene Himalayan state known for its Buddhist heritage, scenic landscapes, and flavorful cuisine influenced by Tibetan and Nepali traditions, with dishes like momos, thukpa, and phagshapa.
